## SECTION 4: FAQ ADDITIONS (Quick Wins)

### Add These Questions to FAQ:

1. **"Why do most businesses go to their bank first?"**
   Answer: Banks are the cheapest capital source and have strong relationships. It's the right first step. When they can't help, we do.

2. **"How do I know if a lender is predatory?"**
   Answer: Red flags include high-pressure sales tactics, unclear rates, fees not disclosed upfront, claims of "guaranteed approval," and ads that seem too good to be true. Good lenders ask questions, explain everything clearly, and let you shop around.

3. **"Why is my tax return asking for my invoices?"**
   Answer: Invoice factoring and some asset-based products focus on invoice quality and customer creditworthiness, not tax returns. We can help even if your tax return shows a loss, as long as your customers are paying reliably.

4. **"How much working capital do profitable companies actually need?"**
   Answer: Profitability and cash flow are different. A $5M profitable company might need $2.5M+ in working capital to fulfill a $10M growth contract. This funds materials, payroll, and inventory before customer payment arrives.

5. **"Can I combine multiple funding sources?"**
   Answer: Yes. "Layered capital" stacks multiple products (AR revolver + term loan + real estate financing) to maximize available funds for growth phases. Example: $1MM + $240K + $550K = $1.79MM total capital.

6. **"What's an 'early payoff discount'?"**
   Answer: Quick funding products often include aggressive early payoff discounts. If you fund at a higher rate but close on a customer contract within weeks, you can refinance into cheaper products. We structure deals expecting this.

7. **"What percentage of businesses actually need working capital?"**
   Answer: Approximately 90%+ of growing companies need working capital at some point in their first 5 years. It might come from family, investors, or lenders, but capital is essential for growth.

8. **"What if I've maxed out my bank line but still need more capital?"**
   Answer: This is where junior/subordinated lending comes in. We have options for businesses that have tapped traditional lenders but still have growth opportunities. These include unsecured term loans, subordinated debt, and layered capital strategies.

9. **"How fast can emergency payroll be funded?"**
   Answer: In urgent situations (24-72 hours), we can secure bridge loans, invoice factoring, or lines of credit against upcoming receivables. Costs range 1-3% for bridge solutions or Prime + 4-12% for term loans.

10. **"Why should I use Serve Funding instead of going direct to a lender?"**
    Answer: Working with a channel-neutral advisor typically reduces your cost of capital by 10-20 percentage points vs. going direct. We have relationships with 30+ lenders, negotiate on your behalf, and steer you away from predatory products.
