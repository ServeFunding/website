## BLOCK 1 — Pricing & the math (≈8 min)

These are the highest-value items for blog/SEO and for the chatbot, because they're the questions every prospect secretly wants answered and almost nobody explains honestly.

**1. The factor-rate-to-APR walkthrough.** *(Gap #12)*
> Pretend I'm a business owner and I just got handed a term sheet that says "1.3 factor rate" or "1.5% discount plus half a point every 10 days." Walk me through, out loud, why that's actually a 50–80% APR. Use real numbers — "okay, say you factor a $100,000 invoice…". This one segment could be a whole blog post.

**2. Why bank money is cheaper — what's actually going on.** *(Gap #13)*
> You always tell people "the bank is the cheapest, real estate gets the lowest rates." But why? In plain English — what does a bank have access to that a non-bank lender doesn't (cost of capital, deposits, regulation, the relationship), and why does that show up as a lower rate? Help a non-finance person understand why your money costs more than a bank's, without it sounding like an apology.

**3. What the 2% success fee actually buys.** *(Gap #14)*
> Do the before/after. "If you go to OnDeck or a factor directly, here's what happens to your rate and your terms. Here's what happens when I bring the same deal to them, because they know me and they know I'll walk." Make the value argument the way you'd make it to a skeptical prospect who's thinking about cutting you out.

**4. The early-payoff / interest-forgiveness mechanic.** *(Gap #15)*
> You mention "100% interest forgiveness on prepay" and "pay it off in two months and you've only paid 6–7%." Walk through one product where that early-payoff discount really matters, with numbers. When should a borrower lean into it, and when does it not help?
