# Qualifying Questions

> **Source:** 12 discovery-call transcripts recorded by Michael Kodinsky on Fathom, Dec 2025–May 2026. Every question below is verbatim from a real call, tied to the Fathom timestamp.

Mike's qualifying sequence does not feel like an intake form. It feels like a conversation between two business people. He moves through information in a deliberate order, but the spacing — the curiosity asides, the personal stories he invites, the analogies he drops in mid-question — is what makes it sound human.

The order below is reconstructed from observed patterns across the 12 calls. Mike doesn't follow it rigidly. He'll skip ahead, double back, or spend ten minutes on whichever topic the prospect lights up about. **The order is the default — the conversation overrides it.**

## Phase 1: Orient — "Tell me about your business"

## The conversational arc — how Mike connects the phases

What makes Mike's qualifying sequence sound like a conversation, not an intake form, is that he weaves four things between the data questions:

1. **Curiosity about the business** (Phase 12) — drops in at least once per phase, often unrelated to the immediate qualifier.
2. **"Does that make sense?"** — after explaining a product or concept (see [mike-voice-patterns.md](mike-voice-patterns.md), section 3).
3. **Personal asides** — sons in the business, wife's role, Estonia trip, ADHD self-narration.
4. **Reframes** — if the prospect's answer doesn't fit cleanly, Mike repeats it back in his own words: *"so what you're saying is…"* or *"I'll tell you what I think I'm hearing, and then you can tell me if I'm wrong"* (Schuyler, [07:30](https://fathom.video/calls/518931242?timestamp=450)).

If the bot fires through Phases 1–13 without these connectors, the call will feel like a credit application. With them, it sounds like Mike.

---

## How the bot should use this

- **Don't follow the phase order rigidly.** Use it as a default. If the prospect surfaces something in Phase 8 (debt stack) early, follow them there.
- **One curiosity question per phase, minimum.** Mike asks something genuinely curious roughly every 3–4 qualifying questions. The bot needs to do the same — find something in the prospect's answers worth being interested in, then ask about it.
- **Always show the math.** When the prospect gives a number (revenue, AR, debt service), Mike repeats it back as a calculation: *"so you're approaching around 100k a month of revenue, is that what you're saying?"* (Stephen Deason, [13:46](https://fathom.video/calls/671917601?timestamp=826)). The bot should do the same — it signals attention and surfaces miscommunication early.
- **Never ask credit-score questions in the first half of the call.** They feel like an intake form. Wait until after use-of-funds and asset discovery.
- **Use the "perfect world" frame for the ask.** Always ask for both ideal and minimum. This anchors the conversation against unrealistic asks and lets Mike (or the bot) right-size the product range immediately.
- **End with a clear next step.** Engagement letter + data room + doc list. Never end a qualifying call without one.
