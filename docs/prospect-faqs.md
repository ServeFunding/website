# Prospect FAQs

> **Source:** 12 discovery-call transcripts recorded by Michael Kodinsky on Fathom, Dec 2025–May 2026. Every question below was actually asked by a prospective Serve Funding client (a real small-business owner, founder, or operator) on a real call. Quotes are verbatim with the Fathom timestamp link preserved.

The bot should treat this as the question library it's most likely to face when talking to a cold or warm prospect. Answer the prospect's actual concern, not the literal words — they almost always come in disguised as something else.

---

## 1. Fees — "How do you get paid? Is that on top of the rate?"

**Frequency:** Asked in nearly every call, usually 30–45 minutes in, after Mike has earned enough trust to be candid.

The fee question is rarely a sticker-shock question. It's a "trust check" — they want to see how transparent Mike will be. Mike volunteers it before they ask in about half of calls. The structure is the same every time: **2% success fee on the funded amount; 25 bps (with a $500 minimum) as a refundable due-diligence retainer; the DD fee comes out of the 2%, never on top.**

**Representative quotes:**

- *"now you get, that's in addition to whatever the rate would be."* — Lynn Chipperfield, [42:12](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=2532)
- *"How do you, how do you guys get paid? I know you're not doing this for. You know, your good looks, you know, but…"* — Daryl Wakefield, [14:52](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=892)
- *"so you guys primarily, Michael, are making money on the success fee. you find the funding, then you guys are going to be that kind of line item."* — Eli Angote, [42:47](https://fathom.video/calls/631676745?timestamp=2567)
- *"How do you get compensated on this?"* — Schuyler Rooke, [12:30](https://fathom.video/calls/518931242?timestamp=750)
- *"so it's, so it's 2.25 on in, oh, sorry."* — Lawson Aschenbach, [36:20](https://fathom.video/calls/579772758?timestamp=2180)
- *"Oh, oh, oh, I'm sorry. Okay, so it's an upfront deal."* — Lawson Aschenbach, [37:12](https://fathom.video/calls/579772758?timestamp=2232)

**What's underneath the question:** They've been burned by brokers who double-dipped (client fee + lender kickback) or who hid a 5–10% origination fee in the term sheet. Mike's answer is always to show the math live, in dollars, on their specific deal size.

## 2. Timing — "How fast can we get the money?"

**Frequency:** Asked in most calls. Usually surfaces after the prospect has just disclosed a hard deadline (a contract date, a payroll Friday, a hotel close date, a vendor invoice past due).

**Representative quotes:**

- *"I'll ask you the question that I, that our CFO would ask me, how soon do we get the money?"* — Lynn Chipperfield, [45:13](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=2713)
- *"And you said that it will take approximately and definitely And So to four weeks to set it up?"* — Lwany Sarabia, [13:57](https://fathom.video/calls/522844430?timestamp=837)
- *"And then if we are thinking about buying equipment, how long do you think is the process to get a loan or a lease?"* — Lwany Sarabia, [14:04](https://fathom.video/calls/522844430?timestamp=844)
- *"how quickly can we get an AR loan, how quickly you can get this done?"* — Schuyler Rooke, [13:21](https://fathom.video/calls/518931242?timestamp=801)
- *"we are at a crunch time now, where if we don't get funded some money this week, I think we're done."* — Rosa Rodriguez (Venicasa), [50:15](https://fathom.video/calls/584950992?timestamp=3015)

**What's underneath the question:** Two distinct flavors. (1) Genuine urgency — they have a real fire drill. (2) Anxious shopping — they want a quick yes-or-no signal. Mike's standard frame: *"the money that comes the fastest, as you already know what I'm going to say next, is probably the most expensive"* ([Lynn, 45:38](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=2738)).

## 3. Product fit — "What kind of loan would this even be?"

**Frequency:** Surfaces in almost every call — but as a confused, half-formed question, not a clean one. Prospects don't know the product names.

**Representative quotes:**

- *"Let me ask you a couple of things just on the products that you said. Obviously, we're looking at working capital for inventory so that we can buy inventory. And I just wonder, find myself wondering, should we be looking for what is strictly a working capital loan that is based on inventory or should we be looking at something, broader line of credit…"* — Lynn Chipperfield, [24:22](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=1462)
- *"for me, being in the dealership industry, I would love to run this like a car dealership. I would love to have a floor plan, if you will, for inventory. And that's kind of like, being in that industry kind of made me realize, well, why can't I do that here?"* — Lawson Aschenbach, [37:45](https://fathom.video/calls/579772758?timestamp=2265)
- *"Do you think it's possible to start with one product and then transition into another product?"* — Daryl Wakefield, [13:00](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=780)
- *"on the factoring product, what is the usual cost?"* — Daryl Wakefield, [04:47](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=287)
- *"is that 2% of the actual collections, or is that on top of, or is that a gross 2%?"* — Daryl Wakefield, [05:03](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=303)
- *"In this particular factoring program, is there a daily sweep for the collections from that particular joint bank account that all has access to, or is there a weekly or a monthly sweep of funds?"* — Daryl Wakefield, [07:12](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=432)
- *"how much, how much are we talking in of like the possible amount that we could, that we could borrow, if we could service the payments?"* — Eli Angote, [34:02](https://fathom.video/calls/631676745?timestamp=2042)
- *"And do they do that customer facing, so the customer's aware that they're in the middle of it all, or is that all done behind the scenes?"* — Chuck Wahr (Lowe & Fletcher), [29:30](https://fathom.video/calls/659542194?timestamp=1770)

**What's underneath the question:** The prospect almost never knows the right name for the product they need. They describe a workflow ("I just need to buy inventory faster") or another industry's product ("a floor plan, like a car dealership"). The bot should resist correcting their terminology and instead match its answer to the workflow they described, then name the product as a footnote.

## 4. Differentiation — "Why are you different from the brokers spamming me?"

**Frequency:** Comes up in ~half of calls, often unprompted. Prospects volunteer how badly they've been burned.

**Representative quotes:**

- *"I will confess to you that I think I made what I think is now a strategic error, a couple of weeks ago, I got an unsolicited email from LendingTree…I'm literally within five minutes, I mean, I mean, don't know, I can't even begin to tell you how many emails I got from all of these lenders, all saying basically the same thing, you know, we can get this money for you in 24 hours… that's not what we're looking for. What we're looking for is a relationship."* — Lynn Chipperfield, [22:00](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=1320)
- *"They're most, I'm in Marietta, Georgia. Okay, most of our calls are from New Jersey, or New York, New York… it's, it's like, almost like something out of a movie, I mean, it's really, the whole M.C.A. world is…"* — Daryl Wakefield, [15:25](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=925)
- *"I just, you need somebody that's like, you know what, I like this horse, he's been around, he knows his deal, you know, let's bet on…"* — Lewis Farsedakis, [42:51](https://fathom.video/calls/672510528?timestamp=2571)
- *"I'd love to, love to a little bit more, heard more about your firm and what you drive towards. I've done some, some elementary or quicker research, but I know that there's more, more to the story than what's ultimately communicated through, through the website. So I'd love to learn a little bit more about, about you."* — Joel Hamann, [02:37](https://fathom.video/calls/640501902?timestamp=157)
- *"I, I'd love to, love to a little bit more, heard more about your firm and what you drive towards…I want to find the best deal, obviously, but I also want to find a really strategic partner. I think that's really important. and I've, I've come, I've been around for a while now, and I've, I've come to believe that it's, who you work with is really important, and I'm just not a transactional person."* — Joel Hamann, [32:26](https://fathom.video/calls/640501902?timestamp=1946)

**What's underneath the question:** They want to know if Mike (or the bot) is going to (a) sell them an MCA at 100%+ APR, (b) ghost them after the close, (c) re-sell their data to other brokers. The framing the bot should use is the same one Mike uses live: *"a non-bank financing advisor… we get our referrals from bankers when folks are turned down by banks"* — that anchor (banker introductions, not internet lead-gen) is the single biggest differentiation signal.

## 5. Process / docs — "What do you need from me?"

**Frequency:** Asked toward the end of most calls, after the prospect has decided they want to move forward.

**Representative quotes:**

- *"Tell me what you need. I'm guessing that you would, you need a bank statement."* — Lynn Chipperfield, [43:02](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=2582)
- *"what, you'll send me, you'll send me an engagement letter for you guys. I can mull that over. That's, that'd be your first move hanging up here?"* — Schuyler Rooke, [15:27](https://fathom.video/calls/518931242?timestamp=927)
- *"just wanted to send me like a full proposal and all that, so I can share that with my partner."* — Frank Tonuzi, [20:18](https://fathom.video/calls/509775187?timestamp=1218)

**What's underneath the question:** Two needs. (1) Logistics — what's the next step. (2) Decision input — they need a doc to take to a co-founder, spouse, or board. Mike's standard answer: *"we would create a data room…we'll send you a list of docs you want to collect…we'll create an executive summary…invite several lenders in"* ([Lynn, 42:35](https://fathom.video/calls/548629615?timestamp=2555)). The bot should never give a generic checklist — it should tailor the list to the product Mike thinks fits.

## 6. Can you beat my existing offer? — "I already have a term sheet…"

**Frequency:** Less common but high-signal. Almost always from a more sophisticated borrower (banker-referred, has been through a process).

**Representative quotes:**

- *"this Valley situation, if, if Chase comes back and says, Hey, we can't do it. Now they're saying that they have something for me… in your overview here, after, you know, stuff I've said, like the offer from Valley being a 1.5 in six months, they'll move it to two, as long as we do everything right. Crime plus one, I have that 30 day buy down, which is a big problem… Is that something that you feel that you can beat?"* — Lawson Aschenbach, [39:48](https://fathom.video/calls/579772758?timestamp=2388)
- *"you have two term sheets. What, what are those like? What, what, do you mind me asking who they are from and what sort of structure they are?"* — (Mike asked Joel) — Joel had a Prospect and Decathlon term sheet, [32:19](https://fathom.video/calls/640501902?timestamp=1939)

**What's underneath the question:** They want the bot to (a) tell them their existing offer is bad so they keep shopping, or (b) confirm their existing offer is good so they can walk. Mike's answer is consistently honest: if the bank offer is cheaper, take it. *"I don't think we would be trying to beat that from a cost. What we're going to deliver is going to be a higher cost of capital, which will come with more flexibility, you know, there's no two ways about it"* ([Lawson, 42:02](https://fathom.video/calls/579772758?timestamp=2522)). The bot should never bluff on rate.

## 7. Hope / fear — "Can you actually help me?"

**Frequency:** Surfaces when the prospect is in distress (MCA stacks, default deadline, cash crunch).

**Representative quotes:**

- *"So, I don't know if you are the company that we can pray and hope that you can help us, or if we just continue doing this."* — Rosa Rodriguez (Venicasa), [16:43](https://fathom.video/calls/584950992?timestamp=1003)
- *"you mute. You know, you're I'll be honest with you, I shouldn't be saying this to somebody who I was looking to potentially get some funding from, but I'm a bit scared right now…"* — Carlos Rodriguez (Venicasa), [55:33](https://fathom.video/calls/584950992?timestamp=3333)
- *"And I don't care, as long as it's not crazy MCA money, because then you never get out of that type of credit."* — Lewis Farsedakis, [43:45](https://fathom.video/calls/672510528?timestamp=2625)

**What's underneath the question:** They want someone to be honest with them about whether their situation is solvable. Mike's tell here is to slow down and use the basement/ladder analogy: *"you're not going from the basement to the first floor with us in one leap, but you're going a few steps up the ladder"* ([Carlos, 32:02](https://fathom.video/calls/584950992?timestamp=1922)). The bot should never promise an outcome it can't deliver — Mike's signature move is to walk away from deals where the math doesn't work, and the bot needs to be willing to do the same.

## 8. Curiosity about Mike personally — "Where are you based? What's your story?"

**Frequency:** Asked in most calls. Prospects warm up by asking about Mike, his team, his location.

**Representative quotes:**

- *"And where, where are you out of? Where are you based?"* — Joel Hamann, [02:32](https://fathom.video/calls/640501902?timestamp=152)
- *"Mike, if you're in Marietta, and, and, and, you know, you can tell we're big boys, we like to eat, you know, we would like to invite you to one of our restaurants…"* — Daryl Wakefield, [16:31](https://fathom.video/calls/641678904?timestamp=991)
- *"What about legal, Michael? Any action you've come across in the legal lawyers who are buying, selling?"* — Eli Angote, [44:48](https://fathom.video/calls/631676745?timestamp=2688)
- *"How long have been going to these meetings? How many have you been to so far?"* — Eli Angote, [52:24](https://fathom.video/calls/631676745?timestamp=3144)
- *"Are you playing? Are you building anything? Are you Vibe coding, Michael, at all?"* — Eli Angote, [41:48](https://fathom.video/calls/631676745?timestamp=2508)

**What's underneath the question:** They're qualifying the human. Mike's answer is always a short story — Marietta GA, family-run, wife is co-founder, two sons in the business, immigrant father, started Serve after a decade on the direct-lender side. The bot should have a one-paragraph version of this ready and offer it any time the prospect asks "tell me about you" — but only once, and never unprompted.

---

## How the bot should use this

- When a prospect asks one of these questions, match the **underlying concern**, not the literal phrasing. A "how do you get paid" question is usually a trust check — answer with the math and the source of the fee, not a price.
- Never invent a quote or attribute one to Mike that isn't in this file. If the bot doesn't have a verbatim answer for a question, it should say so and offer to put the prospect on a real call with Mike.
- The bot's first response to any of these questions should be short. Mike's pattern is to answer briefly, then ask one curious question back ("Does that make sense?" or "What was driving you to ask that?"). Mirror that rhythm.
