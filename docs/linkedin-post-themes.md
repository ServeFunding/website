Serve Funding worked closely with the investors to raise some working capital to support growth and expansion.

However, as we went through the process we saw several negative trends developing since the acquisition took place:

The company had generated over $16MM in revenues and over $1.4MM of net profits in 2024, 

yet ended 2025 at over $500k of net loss, despite top line revenues remaining steady.

Some of this was certainly compounded by the debt service on the acquisition loan...

But as we looked more closely, we discovered the company had not raised its bid figures enough to reflect the growing costs of materials and labor in recent years. 

The client and lender worked closely together to sort through the data and the ownership implemented increases on bid figures across the board.

Through it all our client showed great integrity towards the lender by pushing off the close of the line until they could get their hands fully around their internal issues. 

Then, when they were ready to close, the lender honored their integrity by offering an aggressively priced borrowing-base structured line:

Facility amount:   $1.5MM Asset-Based Revolver
Advance rate:      85% advance on Accounts Receivable
Pricing: 		     Prime + 3% on funds drawn down
Notification:	    NONE
Term:                    24-months with a built-in 90-day exit option
Line fees:             ZERO unused line fees

The line is now driving much-needed liquidity to purchase materials, bridge cash flow gaps, and take on larger contracts—without choking cash flow.

Best of all - both the client and lender exercised tremendous transparency, patience, and forthrightness, vital to kicking off a healthy growth relationship!

Term loan... or line of credit?



Last week, I got a call from a business owner trying to decide between a term loan and a line of credit.  

It made me reflect.  

So often, we look at funding options through the lens of strategy — but it’s really about purpose and trust.  

The difference? It’s simple —  

A line of credit is like riding the wave of your cash flow.  

It’s there for the short-term cycles. You fund an order, receivables come in, you pay it down, and then you’re ready to go again.  

It’s flexible. It’s built for recurring needs, for navigating cyclical business flows.  

But if you’re facing a longer-term challenge — buying out a partner, seizing a big vendor discount, or mobilizing a major contract — that calls for a different approach.  

A term loan acts like a steady anchor, helping you service that debt over time, without the pressure of quick turnaround.  

It’s about clarity. About purpose. About trusting your process and your plan.  

At the end of the day, understanding your use of capital helps you serve your team, your community, and your mission better.  

Because business isn’t just about the numbers. It’s about faith, relationships, and trusting in God's timing.  

Grateful for the opportunity to walk alongside entrepreneurs in this journey.  

If you’re ever unsure, just know that we are here to serve.
