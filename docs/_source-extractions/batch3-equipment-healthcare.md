**The "triangle" analogy for medical A/R factoring — verbatim:**

> [1:13:21](https://fathom.video/calls/592239574?timestamp=4401) Michael Kodinsky: "You're not making any payments at all. It's yours, because they've created an arrangement where they now are going to get paid by Humana. It's like a triangle. It's like a three-part relationship. You, the vendor, and your customer, which is Humana, Medicare, whoever else."

**Full worked example — Humana (30-day pay) and Blue Cross (60-day pay):**

> [1:11:23](https://fathom.video/calls/592239574?timestamp=4283) Michael Kodinsky: "Okay, for example, you invoice Humana $1,000 for a scan... They pay in 30 days. In 30 days, Humana pays to a bank account, which the lender controls, the $1,000. Now, you're still owed the other $100, right? Because it was a 90% advance rate, they held back 10%... > [1:16:35](https://fathom.video/calls/592239574?timestamp=4595) Michael Kodinsky: "In the case of Blue Cross... in 60 days when they pay that invoice, Blue Cross, they still owe you $100, but now two months have gone by, so their fee is now 4%, not 2%, so instead of keeping $20, they're actually keeping $40, and so you're getting $60 back... because you're able to have that money early."
