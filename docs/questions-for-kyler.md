# Questions for Kyler — Content Gaps

> **Purpose:** The docs in this folder (mike-voice-patterns, product-explanations, prospect-faqs, qualifying-questions, objections-and-responses) capture Mike's verbatim voice from 12 discovery calls — incredible material for the chatbot and for content writing. But there are gaps where the transcripts either don't cover a topic, give a half-formed answer, or only show Mike's working draft. This doc lists those gaps as questions Kyler can answer on a recording (or in writing) so we have a fuller corpus to draw from.
>
> **Format:** Answer in Mike's voice — analogies, "does that make sense?", the rhythm. The point isn't a polished script; it's the same kind of raw transcript material as the existing docs, just covering topics the existing calls didn't.

---

## 1. Objections that the existing docs flag as "didn't appear cleanly" in the sample

The `objections-and-responses.md` file notes these objections were anticipated but didn't show up in the 12-call sample. Mike's verbatim answer to each would close the gap:

1. **"I want to think about it / let me get back to you."** What does Mike say in the moment when a prospect goes evasive at the close? Does he push, give them a deadline, just send the engagement letter and wait?
2. **"I want to go direct to a lender — why pay you 2%?"** The fee question is well-covered, but the "I'll cut out the middleman" framing isn't. What's the value argument when the prospect explicitly wants to skip the advisor?
3. **"Send me a few options first, then I'll decide if I want to engage."** The reverse-engagement objection. How does Mike handle prospects who want lender names before signing the agreement?
4. **"Why do I need to sign an exclusive agreement?"** Mike alludes to exclusivity in a few calls but never has the full pitch on why it matters for the client (not just Serve Funding).
5. **"I had a bad broker experience — convince me you're different."** Beyond "boutique, banker-referred," what's the deeper answer when the prospect has been actively burned and is suspicious?

## 2. Product explanations the existing docs only half-cover

The `product-explanations.md` file is strong on AR factoring, MCA, ABL, and consolidation, but thin on the rest. Suggested topics for Kyler to record Mike explaining (or to explain himself):

6. **Equipment leasing + sale-leaseback in plain English.** The existing material has fragments. We need Mike's full explanation with an analogy — what's the "triangle" or "water in a cup" for equipment financing?
7. **PO funding alone (not paired with AR).** The existing material always pairs PO with AR factoring. What does Mike say to someone who only needs PO funding for an international supplier and won't have invoices to factor downstream?
8. **Government contract financing mechanics.** The existing docs don't have Mike's go-to explanation for GSA/DoD/state contracts. What's the standard pitch — retainage, payment cycles, prime-vs-subcontractor distinctions?
9. **SBA 7(a) vs 504 vs Express.** Mike refers SBA out but the website needs a clear "this is what each program is good for" explanation. What's the layperson framing?
10. **Bridge / unsecured term — when and why over secured options.** The existing material has the surgeon/M&A example but doesn't generalize. When does Mike *recommend* unsecured over secured, given it costs more?
11. **Real estate cash-out for working capital.** The Lawson and Carlos calls touch on it but no full explanation. What's the framing, the analogy, the typical structure?

## 3. Pricing transparency — the spots where math is alluded to but not shown

12. **The full factor-rate-to-APR conversion walkthrough.** Mike mentions factor rates and APRs separately but doesn't show a worked example in his own voice. A 5-minute "let me explain why a 1.3 factor is actually 50–80% APR" segment would be enormously useful for blog content (it's the #1 SEO-intent question in this space).
13. **Why bank rates are cheaper.** Mike says "banks are the cheapest" but doesn't say *why*. What's the explanation in plain English — capital cost, regulatory advantage, customer relationship?
14. **What the 2% success fee actually buys.** The fee math is covered, but not the value argument. "You're paying me 2% — here's what you'd pay if you went direct, and here's what I save you" framed as a clean before/after.
15. **The early-payoff discount mechanic in detail.** The capital-strategy page mentions it; the transcripts allude to it. Mike's full explanation, with numbers, would be ideal.
